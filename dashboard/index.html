AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

Enterprise AI adoption is rapidly shifting from experimentation to production workflows, with companies like Notion demonstrating "spec-driven development" where AI agents handle coding while humans focus on specification and thinking. The pattern emerging is that successful AI implementation requires starting with workflow design and customer needs rather than technology capabilities, while governance frameworks are being built retroactively as employees are already using AI tools in regulated functions like finance.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

MY AI Agent - Assembles a 3-10 agent team from a single sentence input to tackle complex tasks.

  • Why it matters: Shows the evolution toward multi-agent orchestration for project workflows, where PMs could delegate entire workstreams to coordinated AI teams rather than single tools.
  • Skill or action: Experiment with breaking down one of your current projects into discrete agent roles and see how you'd prompt coordination between them.

Pixcode - Self-hosted control room for managing AI coding agents across development workflows.

  • Why it matters: Demonstrates the emerging need for agent management platforms as AI development moves beyond individual tools to orchestrated systems.
  • Skill or action: Map out what a "control room" view would look like for your current AI tools and workflows - what would you want to monitor and control centrally?

Hopper - First agentic development environment specifically designed for mainframe/COBOL systems.

  • Why it matters: Shows AI tooling is reaching even legacy enterprise systems, expanding the scope of what can be automated in traditional IT environments.
  • Skill or action: Identify the most legacy or manual system in your organization and research what AI tools are emerging for that domain.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Spec-driven development with AI agents — Why it matters: Notion's approach of writing detailed specifications first, then letting AI handle implementation, creates a new PM workflow where thinking and planning become the core human contribution.

Idea: Customer-back AI engineering — Why it matters: MIT research shows starting with customer needs rather than AI capabilities prevents the 70% value loss most organizations experience with digital investments.

Idea: AI workflow governance after adoption — Why it matters: Finance departments show the pattern where employees adopt AI tools organically, then leadership builds governance retroactively - a model for managing AI rollouts in regulated environments.

Idea: Multi-agent project decomposition — Why it matters: Using AI to automatically assemble specialized agent teams for complex projects could replace traditional resource allocation and team formation processes.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Notion's engineering team has implemented spec-driven development where they write detailed specifications and AI agents execute the coding work, fundamentally changing their development workflow from implementation-focused to specification-focused. Enterprise finance departments are experiencing "quiet insurgency" adoption patterns where employees use AI tools individually while leadership works to impose structure and governance after the fact. OpenAI reports that successful enterprise AI scaling requires focusing on trust, governance, and workflow design rather than just model capabilities, with companies seeing compounding impact only after establishing these foundations.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn specification writing for AI agents - Based on Notion's workflow, the ability to write clear, detailed specifications that AI can execute is becoming a core AI PM skill. Spend 60 minutes this week practicing writing a detailed specification for a small project or feature, focusing on being explicit about requirements, constraints, and success criteria that an AI agent could understand and execute. Start with the Notion article and then try specifying one current task in your workflow with enough detail that you could theoretically hand it to an AI agent.
