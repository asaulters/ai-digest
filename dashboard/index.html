# AI PM Daily Briefing

Today's Signal

The AI tooling landscape is rapidly specializing into domain-specific orchestration layers, with new products emerging for code management, service delivery, and content workflows. Today's releases show a clear trend toward "agentic" AI systems that don't just assist but actively coordinate complex multi-step processes - a shift that fundamentally changes how we think about project management from task coordination to AI orchestration.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Baton - Orchestrates multiple AI coding agents working together on development tasks.

  • Why it matters: Shows the evolution from single AI assistants to multi-agent coordination systems, which is the future of AI PM workflows.
  • Skill to build: Learn multi-agent prompt design and understand how to break complex projects into agent-coordinated subtasks.

Nitro by Rocketlane - AI agents specifically designed for service delivery and client project management.

  • Why it matters: First wave of AI tools purpose-built for professional services workflows, showing how AI PMs need to think vertically.
  • Skill to build: Study service delivery automation patterns and how to map client-facing processes to AI agent capabilities.

WP Copilot - Agentic AI system built specifically for WordPress management and operations.

  • Why it matters: Demonstrates how AI products are becoming platform-native rather than generic, requiring deeper integration thinking.
  • Skill to build: Understand platform-specific AI integration patterns and API orchestration for domain expertise.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Multi-agent code orchestration for project delivery — Why it matters: Shows how AI PMs will need to design agent coordination workflows rather than just managing human teams.

Idea: Service delivery automation with client-facing AI agents — Why it matters: Demonstrates how AI can handle the entire service lifecycle, not just internal automation tasks.

Idea: Platform-native AI integration for content management — Why it matters: Reveals the importance of deep domain integration over generic AI tool adoption.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Companies are moving beyond simple AI assistants to building orchestration layers that coordinate multiple specialized AI agents. Rocketlane's focus on service delivery shows established players are building AI-native versions of their core workflows rather than adding AI as a feature. The academic research interest in software development impact suggests organizations are now measuring and optimizing AI integration systematically rather than experimenting ad-hoc.

Skill to Build This Week

Multi-agent prompt orchestration - With tools like Baton showing agent coordination becoming mainstream, spend 60 minutes learning how to design prompt chains where multiple AI agents hand off work to each other. Start with Claude or ChatGPT's custom GPT feature to create two agents that pass a project brief back and forth, refining it iteratively. This skill will be essential as AI PM work shifts from managing humans to orchestrating AI systems.
