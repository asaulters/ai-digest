# Daily AI PM Briefing

Today's Signal

The AI tooling landscape is shifting toward self-adapting, workflow-aware systems that learn how users work rather than forcing rigid implementations. Dan Shipper's analysis points to a critical paradox: as AI automation increases, we're seeing more humans in the loop and more work being created, making product managers and designers more valuable than ever as orchestrators of human-AI collaboration.

  • Skill or action: Study how workflow observation and pattern recognition could be applied to your current project management processes

  • Skill or action: Explore how agent frameworks could automate routine project documentation and status reporting tasks

What The Leaders Are Doing

Dan Shipper predicts that most knowledge work will happen inside AI coding environments like Codex or Claude, but emphasizes that every AI agent still needs human oversight. Leaders are positioning PMs and designers as more valuable, not less, because they orchestrate the human-AI collaboration. Companies are moving away from CLI-based workflows toward more integrated, conversational interfaces that require product thinking to implement effectively.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn basic AI agent architecture and token economics. The articles highlight that understanding how AI agents consume resources and self-adapt is becoming critical for anyone building AI products. Start by reading the "token-burn problem" article and spending 30 minutes experimenting with a simple AI agent framework to understand the cost and efficiency considerations that will drive your product decisions.
