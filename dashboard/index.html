AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

AI systems are evolving from stateless tools to persistent, memory-enabled agents that can maintain context across sessions—a fundamental shift that's reshaping how we build AI products. Meanwhile, industry leaders are warning that traditional PM roles are being "collapsed" by AI, pushing professionals toward more technical, hands-on building skills rather than coordination-focused work.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

BuildBuild - AI-native ERP system specifically designed for construction project management workflows.

  • Why it matters: Shows vertical-specific AI applications moving beyond generic tools into complex operational workflows like resource planning and compliance tracking.
  • Skill or action: Research how industry-specific AI tools handle regulatory compliance and multi-stakeholder coordination—patterns you can apply to other domains.

ContextPool - Provides persistent memory layers for AI coding agents to maintain context between sessions.

  • Why it matters: Addresses the core limitation of stateless LLMs in ongoing project work where context accumulates over time.
  • Skill or action: Experiment with session management in your AI workflows—start tracking what context you manually re-provide to AI tools daily.

Open Comet - Autonomous AI browser agent that can perform research tasks and navigate web interfaces independently.

  • Why it matters: Represents the shift from AI as a chat interface to AI as an autonomous task executor for research and data gathering workflows.
  • Skill or action: Map out 2-3 repetitive research tasks in your current role that could potentially be automated by browser agents.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Persistent AI coding assistants with project memory — Why it matters: Could transform documentation workflows by maintaining context of decisions, requirements changes, and technical debt across project lifecycle.

Idea: Vertical AI ERPs replacing horizontal project tools — Why it matters: Shows the opportunity to build AI-first workflow tools that understand domain-specific compliance and operational patterns rather than generic task management.

Idea: AI memory systems as knowledge graphs vs. search databases — Why it matters: Better memory architecture could enable AI assistants that truly understand project relationships and dependencies rather than just retrieving similar documents.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Keith Rabois (Khosla Ventures) is actively reshaping hiring frameworks around AI capabilities, moving away from traditional PM coordination roles toward "barrels" (builders who can directly execute) rather than "ammunition" (coordinators). Construction companies are adopting AI-native ERP systems that integrate compliance and operational workflows from the ground up. Engineering teams are implementing persistent memory systems for their AI coding workflows rather than treating each interaction as isolated.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn memory system design for AI workflows. The convergence of multiple articles on AI memory suggests this is becoming a critical competency for AI product work. Traditional RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) isn't sufficient—you need to understand how to architect systems that build and maintain context over time. Start by reading the "Stop Treating AI Memory Like a Search Problem" article and then experiment with designing a simple memory layer for one of your regular AI workflows, focusing on how information should accumulate and connect rather than just be stored and retrieved.
