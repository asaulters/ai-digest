AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

The enterprise AI adoption story is splitting into two tracks: foundational data infrastructure overhauls and practical workflow automation. While companies struggle with messy data foundations that block meaningful AI deployment, forward-thinking teams are already automating complex operational workflows—from engineering sprint management to supply chain coordination—using AI agents that can reason through multi-step processes.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Kōan - An observability platform that lets you see inside AI agents' reasoning processes, tool calls, and decision-making paths.

  • Why it matters: Essential for AI PMs debugging and optimizing agent workflows; visibility into agent behavior is critical for production reliability.
  • Skill or action: Learn observability fundamentals for AI systems—start with understanding how to trace and debug LLM reasoning chains.

Blueprint by Imbue - Converts informal prompts into structured, detailed plans specifically designed for coding agents.

  • Why it matters: Bridges the gap between human intent and agent execution; critical for scaling development workflows with AI assistance.
  • Skill or action: Practice prompt structuring techniques—experiment with converting vague requests into step-by-step agent instructions.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Autonomous SDLC workflows beyond basic ticket tracking — Why it matters: Atlassian is moving past static boards toward AI that autonomously manages dependencies, resource allocation, and cross-team coordination.

Idea: AI agent supply chain orchestration — Why it matters: Choco's success shows how agents can manage complex, multi-party workflows with real-time decision-making across vendors and logistics.

Idea: Prompt-to-plan automation for technical workflows — Why it matters: Blueprint demonstrates how to systematically convert informal requirements into executable technical specifications at scale.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Atlassian is rebuilding their core project management platform around autonomous AI workflows that operate independently of traditional sprint boards. Choco has deployed AI agents to handle real-time food distribution decisions, managing vendor relationships and logistics coordination without human intervention. Enterprise data teams are prioritizing infrastructure rebuilds specifically to enable AI adoption, recognizing that data quality is the primary blocker to meaningful AI deployment.

Skill to Build This Week

AI Agent Observability and Debugging - With tools like Kōan emerging and companies deploying agents in production, understanding how to monitor, trace, and debug AI agent behavior is becoming critical for AI PMs. Spend 60 minutes learning how to trace LLM reasoning chains and identify failure points in multi-step agent workflows. Start with OpenAI's function calling documentation and practice logging intermediate reasoning steps in a simple agent workflow.
