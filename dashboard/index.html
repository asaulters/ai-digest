AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

Enterprise AI adoption is hitting real operational constraints as companies like Uber cap AI tool usage after burning through budgets 4x faster than expected. Meanwhile, the platform wars are intensifying—Atlassian is building LLM infrastructure directly into development workflows, and we're seeing a clear split between companies trying to control AI costs versus those doubling down on AI-native operations.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Atlassian Forge LLMs - Atlassian now hosts LLMs directly in their development platform for building AI-powered apps within Jira/Confluence ecosystems.

  • Why it matters: Eliminates the complexity of managing external AI APIs for workplace automation and could become the standard for enterprise AI app development.
  • Skill/action: Learn Atlassian's Forge platform development basics—this could become a key differentiator for AI PMs working in enterprise environments.

Perplexity Personal Computer for Windows - AI agents that can run across local files and applications on Windows systems.

  • Why it matters: Represents the shift toward AI agents that can actually interact with existing desktop workflows rather than just web-based tools.
  • Skill/action: Test local AI agents on your own Windows workflows to understand the capabilities and limitations of desktop AI automation.

Chloe by Close - AI agent built directly into CRM that autonomously works leads.

  • Why it matters: Shows how AI agents are moving from assistive tools to autonomous workflow executors in sales operations.
  • Skill/action: Audit your current CRM workflows to identify which repetitive tasks could be candidates for AI agent automation.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: AI agents for design system compliance — Why it matters: Atlassian is using AI to automatically apply their design system rules, which could be replicated for any standardized documentation or compliance workflow.

Idea: Cost-aware AI agent deployment — Why it matters: Uber's budget overrun shows the need for built-in cost controls and usage monitoring in any AI workflow implementation.

Idea: Local file AI processing — Why it matters: Running AI agents on local files removes data privacy concerns and enables automation of document-heavy compliance workflows.

Idea: 24/7 AI customer support scaling — Why it matters: Travelers' claims assistant shows how AI can handle peak demand scenarios that would typically require hiring temporary staff.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Endava is restructuring their entire software delivery process around AI agents rather than just adding AI tools to existing workflows—they're building "AI-native culture" from the ground up. Travelers deployed AI claims assistance countrywide, showing enterprise-scale AI deployment in regulated industries. Uber is implementing strict AI usage caps across all employees, indicating that cost management is becoming as important as AI capabilities. Companies are moving from experimentation to operational discipline around AI spend and ROI measurement.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn AI agent boundaries and guardrails design. Based on multiple articles discussing what AI agents should/shouldn't do autonomously, this is becoming critical for AI PMs. The industry is moving past "can we make AI do this?" to "should we make AI do this?" Start with the Towards Data Science article on AI agent limitations, then draft guardrails for one workflow in your current role. This skill separates AI PMs who build sustainable systems from those who create expensive, unreliable automation.
