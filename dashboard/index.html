AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

AI workflows are rapidly shifting from experimental prompting to production-grade automation systems, with enterprise adoption accelerating across core business functions. The key development is the emergence of "repeatable AI workflows" — structured systems that move beyond ad-hoc ChatGPT interactions to scalable, integrated processes for project management, customer operations, and compliance tasks. This represents the maturation of AI from tool to infrastructure, with clear implications for AI PMs who need to architect sustainable, cost-controlled systems rather than just deploy models.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Anthropic's product team is pioneering rapid iteration cycles by building products before models are fully ready, using "launch room" processes that prioritize speed over traditional strategic planning. Enterprise organizations are moving beyond pilot programs — half of companies now use AI in at least three business functions, focusing on finance, supply chains, HR, and customer operations. Leading AI PMs are shifting from prompt engineering to workflow architecture, designing systems that integrate data pipelines, automation triggers, and agent orchestration. Tech companies are implementing urgent cost controls around AI token spending while maintaining production AI capabilities.

Skill to Build This Week

Agent workflow design and orchestration — specifically learning how to architect multi-step AI processes with proper error handling, cost controls, and business integration points. This is becoming the core differentiator for AI PMs as the field moves from experimental prompting to production systems. Start with OpenAI's Academy content on workspace agents and automations, then practice designing a simple workflow that combines a trigger (like a project milestone), an AI processing step (like generating a status report), and an integration point (like updating a project management tool). Focus on defining clear input/output contracts and failure modes for each step.
