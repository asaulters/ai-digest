# AI PM Daily Briefing

Today's Signal

The bottleneck in tech product development is fundamentally shifting from engineering capacity constraints to how effectively teams can integrate AI into workflows and manage the resulting acceleration. Companies are moving beyond point solutions to create comprehensive AI adoption frameworks with internal tooling, skills marketplaces, and value-based commercial models that can keep pace with AI-driven change.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Donely Knowledge Layer — Platform for hosting and managing AI employees powered by OpenClaw.

*Why it matters:* Represents the shift toward AI workforce management as a core product capability rather than ad-hoc automation.

*Skill or action:* Research how to architect knowledge layers that can scale across multiple AI agents in your organization.

Stage CLI — Command-line tool that makes it easier to review AI-generated code changes locally before committing.

*Why it matters:* Addresses the critical workflow gap between AI code generation and human review/approval processes.

*Skill or action:* Set up Stage CLI in your development workflow to understand how AI-generated changes can be systematically reviewed.

Agent Harness Kit — Scaffolding framework for building multi-agent workflows that's provider-agnostic and supports MCP (Model Context Protocol).

*Why it matters:* Provides standardized architecture for complex AI workflows that don't lock you into specific AI providers.

*Skill or action:* Study the MCP specification and experiment with building a simple multi-agent workflow using this framework.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: AI adoption gamification with quests, token leaderboards, and skills marketplaces — Why it matters: Sendbird's approach shows how to drive organizational AI adoption through product thinking rather than top-down mandates.

Idea: Portable knowledge layers with automated context updates — Why it matters: Solves the critical problem of keeping AI systems current with real-time information without manual maintenance overhead.

Idea: Value-based commercial models that adjust to AI velocity — Why it matters: Traditional fixed pricing breaks down when AI accelerates delivery timelines unpredictably.

Idea: AIOps integration patterns combining asset management, logging, and tracing — Why it matters: Shows how AI can unify previously siloed operational intelligence for faster incident resolution.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Sendbird's CEO built an internal AI adoption system as a product, complete with quest mechanics and token economies, with their marketing team now shipping live swag stores in one day with zero engineers. Atlassian is restructuring their entire commercial model around "Flex" pricing to accommodate AI-driven development velocity. Enterprise teams are increasingly setting up dedicated AI code review workflows rather than treating AI-generated code as regular contributions. Meta is reportedly assigning engineers to data labeling work ahead of layoffs, signaling a shift in how companies view engineering talent allocation in the AI era.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn the Model Context Protocol (MCP) — This emerging standard for AI agent communication is becoming critical infrastructure for multi-agent workflows. Start with the MCP specification documentation and build a simple agent that can share context with another agent. This matters now because provider-agnostic AI architectures are becoming table stakes for avoiding vendor lock-in as the AI landscape shifts rapidly.
