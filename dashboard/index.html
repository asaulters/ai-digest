AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

Production AI systems are moving from experimentation to systematic evaluation and monitoring, with enterprise teams building formal measurement frameworks and reliability tooling. The focus has shifted to making AI agents actually work consistently in business workflows rather than just demonstrating capabilities—reflected in new evaluation harnesses, analytics platforms, and state machine approaches for reliability.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Open Computer Use — Open-source MCP (Model Context Protocol) that lets AI agents interact with computer interfaces directly.

  • Why it matters: Enables AI agents to automate desktop workflows that current API-based tools can't reach, opening new automation possibilities for project management tasks.
  • Skill or action: Experiment with desktop automation use cases in your current workflows to identify where computer control could eliminate manual steps.

Enjo Help Center — AI tool that automatically builds help centers by learning from team knowledge and interactions.

  • Why it matters: Shows how AI can handle documentation workflows that typically require significant PM coordination and maintenance.
  • Skill or action: Audit your current documentation processes to identify knowledge that could be automatically captured and structured.

Open Browser Use — Open-source browser automation specifically designed for local AI agents.

  • Why it matters: Provides a foundation for building AI agents that can handle web-based workflows without relying on APIs.
  • Skill or action: Map out browser-based tasks in your workflow that could benefit from AI automation.

Voker Analytics — Analytics platform specifically designed for monitoring and measuring AI agent performance.

  • Why it matters: Addresses the critical gap in understanding how AI agents perform in production environments.
  • Skill or action: Learn about AI observability metrics and how to measure agent effectiveness in business processes.

Statewright — Visual state machine framework designed to make AI agents more reliable and predictable.

  • Why it matters: Tackles the reliability problem that prevents AI agents from being trusted in critical business workflows.
  • Skill or action: Study state machine concepts and how they apply to making AI workflows more robust.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: 12-metric evaluation framework for AI agents — Why it matters: Provides a systematic approach to measure AI agent performance across retrieval, generation, behavior, and production health in enterprise deployments.

Idea: AI alert grouping for incident management — Why it matters: Atlassian saved 839 hours in 28 days by using AI to automatically group and prioritize alerts, showing concrete ROI in operational workflows.

Idea: AI-generated opportunity solution trees in product management — Why it matters: Demonstrates how AI can augment structured product thinking frameworks rather than replacing human judgment.

Idea: Embedded AI builders for SaaS products — Why it matters: Shows how product teams can integrate AI capabilities directly into their platforms rather than building separate AI tools.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Atlassian is systematically deploying AI across operational workflows, with measurable results like saving 839 hours through AI alert grouping and accelerating frontend development from quarters to weeks with RovoDev. Product teams are building evaluation harnesses based on 100+ enterprise AI agent deployments, indicating mature measurement approaches are becoming standard practice. Technical decision makers are following "secular trends supported by analysts" rather than experimenting independently, suggesting AI adoption is moving through established enterprise adoption patterns.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn AI agent evaluation metrics and frameworks. The industry is moving beyond "does it work sometimes" to systematic measurement of AI agent performance. With evaluation harnesses becoming critical for production deployments, understanding how to measure retrieval accuracy, generation quality, agent behavior, and system health is becoming essential for AI PMs. Start with the 12-metric framework mentioned in the Towards Data Science article and practice applying these metrics to any AI tools you're currently using or evaluating.
