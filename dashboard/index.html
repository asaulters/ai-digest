AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

The biggest shift happening right now is the enterprise readiness gap for agentic AI—while 85% of organizations want to implement AI agents within three years, 76% admit their current operations can't support it. This disconnect reveals that the next competitive advantage isn't having AI tools, but having the organizational design, workflows, and judgment to deploy them effectively at scale.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

CometChat Skills - Enables voice and video calling capabilities for coding agents and AI systems.

  • Why it matters: As AI agents become more collaborative, real-time communication bridges the gap between automated workflows and human oversight in project management.
  • Skill: Learn how to design handoff protocols between AI agents and human stakeholders in your workflows.

AgenticCalling AI - Gives AI agents the ability to make phone calls autonomously.

  • Why it matters: Extends AI automation into traditionally human-only touchpoints like client check-ins, status updates, or stakeholder communications.
  • Skill: Map your current communication workflows to identify where automated calling could reduce manual coordination overhead.

Octolane - Self-driving AI CRM with conversational interface.

  • Why it matters: Shows the evolution toward AI systems that manage complex relationship data while maintaining natural interaction patterns for project teams.
  • Skill: Practice designing conversational interfaces for internal tools by prototyping simple chatbot workflows for your current project management needs.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Deterministic loops around AI agents for document processing — Why it matters: Instead of using LLMs as black boxes, build structured workflows where AI handles discrete tasks within human-designed quality control loops.

Idea: AI-first support as revenue driver — Why it matters: Leadership Circle achieved 71% AI resolution rates by treating AI not as cost reduction but as a growth enabler, applicable to internal project support workflows.

Idea: Organizational design for agentic readiness — Why it matters: The gap between AI ambition and execution capability suggests that workflow redesign and change management are becoming core AI product skills.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Leadership Circle transformed their entire support function to AI-first operations, achieving 71% AI resolution rates and 65% reduction in manual tickets while serving 3 million customers across 17 languages. They shifted from viewing AI support as a cost center to positioning it as a revenue driver. Meanwhile, enterprise leaders are recognizing that their biggest bottleneck isn't AI capability but organizational readiness—most lack the people, processes, and workflows to support agentic AI implementations.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn deterministic workflow design for AI agents. The pattern of building structured loops around LLMs (rather than treating them as magical problem solvers) is becoming critical for reliable AI product development. Spend 60 minutes this week mapping one of your current manual processes into discrete steps, then identify which steps could be AI-automated while maintaining human checkpoints and quality controls. Start with a simple document review or data validation workflow.
