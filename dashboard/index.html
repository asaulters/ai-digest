AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

The big story today is the enterprise shift from prompt-based AI tools to integrated workflow automation, with real companies like Endava and Uber demonstrating both the massive potential and cost management challenges at scale. We're seeing a maturation moment where AI product success depends less on individual tool capabilities and more on workflow integration, cost control, and governance frameworks that keep AI agents effective within business constraints.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Curata - A shared workspace platform for AI agents and humans to collaborate on tasks together.

  • Why it matters: Addresses the core AI PM challenge of orchestrating human-AI workflows rather than just deploying isolated AI tools.
  • Skill or action: Study how collaborative AI workspaces handle task handoffs between agents and humans.

Perplexity Personal Computer for Windows - Enables AI agents to run across local files and applications on Windows systems.

  • Why it matters: Represents the shift toward AI agents that can automate entire desktop workflows, not just web-based tasks.
  • Skill or action: Explore local AI agent capabilities and understand security/privacy implications for enterprise deployments.

Cost.dev - A platform that makes AI agents cost-aware and cheaper to operate by optimizing model usage and routing.

  • Why it matters: Directly addresses the cost management crisis enterprises like Uber are facing with AI tool proliferation.
  • Skill or action: Learn cost optimization strategies for LLM operations and agent workflows.

Open Code Review - An AI-powered CLI tool for automated code review processes.

  • Why it matters: Shows how AI can be embedded into existing developer workflows rather than replacing them entirely.
  • Skill or action: Understand how to integrate AI tools into existing technical workflows and approval processes.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Agent governance frameworks that define what AI can and cannot do autonomously — Why it matters: Essential for scaling AI in enterprise environments while maintaining compliance and risk management.

Idea: Cost-aware AI routing that automatically selects the most efficient model for each task — Why it matters: Prevents budget overruns while maintaining performance, addressing a key challenge in AI product scaling.

Idea: Hybrid human-AI workspaces where tasks flow seamlessly between people and agents — Why it matters: Maximizes both human expertise and AI efficiency rather than forcing an either-or choice.

Idea: Local AI agents integrated with existing desktop applications and file systems — Why it matters: Enables workflow automation without requiring data to leave corporate environments.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Endava is building an AI-native software delivery culture by integrating AI agents directly into their development workflows using ChatGPT Enterprise and Codex, focusing on acceleration rather than replacement of human developers.

Uber hit a reality check on AI costs, burning through their entire 2024 AI budget in four months and now implementing usage caps across all AI tools, demonstrating that even tech giants need governance frameworks for AI adoption.

Neta/Engineering Group reduced ticket escalations by 35% by connecting service management, knowledge bases, and AI on a single platform (Jira Service Management + Rovo), showing the power of unified AI workflow platforms.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn AI agent governance and constraint setting - Understanding how to define boundaries for autonomous AI systems is becoming critical as enterprises move beyond experimentation. The combination of Uber's cost overruns and the focus on "what AI agents should never do" shows this is an urgent skill gap. Start with the Towards Data Science article on AI agent constraints and spend 30-60 minutes mapping out governance frameworks for a real workflow you manage today.
