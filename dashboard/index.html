AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

The industry is at an inflection point where AI tooling is rapidly commoditizing while internal enterprise adoption remains surprisingly stagnant—even at tech giants like Google. The real opportunity isn't in the AI models themselves, but in the workflow orchestration layer that connects business tools and automates complex, multi-step processes without requiring engineering resources.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Softr AI Co-Builder - Builds functional business applications through conversational AI prompting rather than traditional no-code interfaces.

  • Why it matters: Demonstrates the shift from drag-and-drop builders to natural language app creation, potentially eliminating the learning curve for internal tool development.
  • Skill or action: Experiment with describing business workflows in natural language to understand how AI interprets process requirements.

Claude Cowork - Connects business tools (email, Slack, calendars) and creates AI skills that automate cross-platform workflows without coding.

  • Why it matters: This is the automation layer that AI PMs need to understand—bridging individual AI capabilities into end-to-end business processes.
  • Skill or action: Map out your current tool stack and identify 2-3 repetitive cross-platform workflows that could benefit from AI orchestration.

Cloudflare Agent Cloud with OpenAI - Enterprise platform for building, deploying, and scaling AI agents for real-world business tasks.

  • Why it matters: Shows how AI infrastructure is maturing from experimentation to production-ready enterprise deployment with security and scale built-in.
  • Skill or action: Learn the difference between AI assistants and AI agents—agents can take actions across systems, not just provide information.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: AI-powered customer signal aggregation — Why it matters: Intercom's approach of using AI to identify patterns across individual customer interactions could be applied to project feedback, stakeholder input, and requirements gathering.

Idea: Zero-code workflow automation between business tools — Why it matters: Claude Cowork's model shows how AI can eliminate the technical barrier to connecting disparate systems that project managers typically struggle to integrate.

Idea: Email-to-action workflow automation — Why it matters: HumanInbox represents a pattern where AI doesn't just read and summarize communications, but can trigger actions and updates across project management systems.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Atlassian is running internal "AI Builders Week" programs to help their own teams adopt AI tools, recognizing that transformation requires structured internal enablement, not just tool access. They're also identifying warning signs when AI investments don't deliver ROI—focusing on connected systems rather than fragmented tool adoption.

Google's internal AI adoption mirrors most enterprises: 20% power users, 20% refusers, 60% still figuring it out. This suggests even AI-forward companies struggle with change management and workflow integration.

Intercom is using a "swarm model" where they manually solve customer problems at scale first, then extract patterns to build automated features—treating human workflows as the blueprint for AI automation.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn workflow orchestration thinking - Understanding how to map business processes across multiple tools and identify automation opportunities is becoming more valuable than prompt engineering alone. The articles show that the real AI PM opportunity is in the integration layer, not the models.

Starting point: Take one repetitive workflow you do weekly (like status reporting or stakeholder updates) and map out every tool, input, and decision point involved. This is the foundation for understanding where AI automation can add value.
