AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

The AI product space is rapidly maturing with enterprise teams now focusing on practical implementation over experimentation. Companies are hitting token spending limits and scrambling to optimize costs, while product teams like Anthropic's are redefining traditional PM workflows to move at AI speed. The gap between AI capability and user adoption reveals a critical insight: successful AI products require solving workflow problems, not just adding AI features.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Anthropic's product team has fundamentally restructured their PM role around AI development cycles, building products before models are ready and using "launch room" processes to move faster than traditional product development allows. They're prioritizing speed over traditional strategy frameworks, recognizing that AI product cycles require different planning approaches.

Companies across 15+ tech organizations are scrambling to control exploding AI token costs, with many implementing spending caps and optimization strategies. This suggests that AI PMs need to become fluent in cost management and resource optimization as core competencies, not just product feature design.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn trigger-based automation design using OpenAI's Codex automation framework. Why now: The ability to design AI workflows that run on schedules and triggers is becoming essential as teams move from manual AI interactions to systematic AI-augmented processes. Starting point: Spend 30 minutes mapping one recurring manual task in your current role (like weekly status reports or compliance checks) and design how it could be automated with triggers and AI generation.
