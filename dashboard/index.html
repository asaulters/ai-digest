AI Intelligence Briefing

Today's Signal

AI tooling is accelerating to the point where traditional development bottlenecks are disappearing, but this is creating new challenges around "vibe-based" development and maintaining product discipline. The most interesting development is companies like Sendbird turning AI adoption itself into a gamified product experience, while established players like Atlassian are fundamentally restructuring their commercial models to accommodate AI-driven organizational change.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Bagel AI - AI-powered product intelligence platform for product and GTM teams to analyze user behavior and market trends.

  • Why it matters: Provides AI-native analytics for product managers who need to understand how AI features perform and drive adoption
  • Skill/action: Learn how AI can augment traditional product analytics by exploring user behavior pattern recognition

Basedash MCP server - Data analysis tool that integrates with existing AI workflows to provide database insights across multiple AI platforms.

  • Why it matters: Enables AI PMs to query and analyze data directly within AI tools rather than switching contexts to separate dashboards
  • Skill/action: Understand Model Context Protocol (MCP) architecture for building AI tool integrations

Forge - React toolkit specifically designed for AI application development with pre-built components and patterns.

  • Why it matters: Accelerates AI product prototyping and reduces the technical barrier for AI PMs to build demos and MVPs
  • Skill/action: Familiarize yourself with React-based AI component libraries to speed up product validation

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: Gamified AI adoption with quest systems and token economies — Why it matters: Transforms internal AI tool rollouts from training programs into engagement-driven product experiences that drive measurable behavior change.

Idea: AI-augmented incident resolution with unified asset, log, and trace intelligence — Why it matters: Shows how AI can orchestrate multiple data sources in real-time for operational workflows, reducing MTTR and improving compliance documentation.

Idea: Value-based commercial models that flex with AI-driven organizational change — Why it matters: Demonstrates how AI adoption requires rethinking pricing structures to accommodate rapid scaling and changing team dynamics.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Sendbird's CEO has productized AI adoption internally, creating quest systems, token leaderboards, and rapid deployment capabilities that let their marketing team ship live stores in one day without engineering support. OpenAI's enterprise research shows frontier companies are moving beyond basic AI implementation to building "Codex-powered agentic workflows" that create sustainable competitive advantages. Atlassian is restructuring their entire commercial model with "Flex" pricing specifically to help enterprise customers adapt to AI-driven organizational changes, recognizing that traditional seat-based models don't work when AI changes how teams scale.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn Model Context Protocol (MCP) architecture and implementation patterns. Based on the Basedash MCP server release and the broader trend toward AI tool integration, understanding how to connect AI systems with existing data sources and workflows is becoming essential for AI PMs. MCP is emerging as a standard for AI tool interoperability, and knowing how to design and implement these connections will differentiate you in building practical AI products.

Starting point: Read Anthropic's MCP documentation and build a simple server that connects ChatGPT to a basic data source like a CSV file or database.
