# AI PM Daily Briefing

Today's Signal

The AI security research landscape is experiencing a quality shift that mirrors what's happening across AI tooling — we're moving from obvious "slop" to genuinely useful outputs that require human judgment to evaluate. This transition from quantity-over-quality to sophisticated-but-still-imperfect AI assistance is exactly what AI PMs need to prepare for across all workflows, where the challenge shifts from filtering out bad AI to making good decisions about increasingly capable AI outputs.

Tools & Releases Worth Knowing

Travel Hacking Toolkit — An open-source AI-powered tool for points search and trip planning that gained significant traction on Hacker News.

  • Why it matters: Demonstrates how AI can handle complex, multi-variable optimization problems (points, routes, timing) that mirror resource allocation challenges in project management.
  • Skill or action: Study how it structures complex search and optimization workflows — these patterns apply to vendor selection, resource planning, and schedule optimization.

Influcio — AI marketing agent for managing influencer campaigns from end-to-end.

  • Why it matters: Shows AI agents moving into campaign management workflows with multiple stakeholders and approval processes.
  • Skill or action: Analyze how it handles multi-party coordination and approval workflows — core skills for AI-augmented project management.

Workflow & Use Case Ideas

Idea: AI-compressed research workflows — Atlassian's approach to using AI (Rovo) to condense weeks of research into hours while preserving human judgment in the final analysis.

Why it matters: This is the exact pattern AI PMs need for competitive analysis, user research synthesis, and technical discovery phases.

Idea: Quality-filtered AI security reporting — Linux kernel maintainers are now receiving genuinely useful AI-generated security reports after months of filtering out low-quality submissions.

Why it matters: Shows how to evolve from rejecting AI outputs wholesale to building systems that can identify and act on high-quality AI analysis.

What The Leaders Are Doing

Linux kernel maintainers like Greg Kroah-Hartman and cURL's Daniel Stenberg are actively integrating AI-generated security research into their workflows, spending hours daily processing what they describe as "really good" AI-generated reports. They've moved from dismissing AI security research as "slop" to treating it as a legitimate input that requires serious evaluation time. This represents a major workflow shift for critical infrastructure projects.

Skill to Build This Week

Learn prompt engineering for research synthesis — Based on Atlassian's research compression workflow, spend 60 minutes this week learning how to structure prompts that maintain research rigor while dramatically accelerating synthesis. Start with Atlassian's blog post on their Rovo research process, then practice on a real project by having AI help you synthesize 3-5 sources on a topic you're currently researching, focusing on preserving the nuance and judgment calls that make research valuable.
